FIRST THINGS FIRST

More than likely you are thinking of starting a Dignity chapter because you feel some need for opportunities to have regular contact with gay and lesbian Catholics whose faith is an important part of their lives. Many gay and lesbian people feel that some contact with the Catholic Church is important, but they feel alienated in some way from the local church. Perhaps they need a safe place where they can find accepting and affirming ministry, which they cannot find elsewhere. Your group could be instrumental in fulfilling their needs as well as your own by beginning a chapter.

You may wonder why you should form a Dignity chapter instead of just any group. There are many advantages to being a Dignity chapter, not the least of which is the DIGNITY name itself. Our organization is well known in gay/lesbian and Catholic Church communities and can provide you with an immediate identity in your locale. In addition, you can receive support for your efforts through contact with the National Director in your region and the Regional Coordinator in your area and nearby chapters and through attendance at Regional and National meetings. Being a part of a larger family is important for the leaders of smaller, more isolated groups who find refreshment and energy from meeting their sisters and brothers in Dignity. All members of your chapter would also profit by receiving our excellent National Journal and having connection with other like-minded people throughout the country. Being members of Dignity can make them feel part of a larger endeavor that can have a greater impact on the Church and on peoples' lives than any other local group can have by itself.

Perhaps you are sure now that the kind of group you want to start would be useful and that a connection with Dignity would be desirable. You may wonder where to start. No doubt you have a few friends who feel the way you do and might be happy to begin a chapter. Find a place for your first meeting, get out the news by word of mouth and do some publicity. (Helpful hints on publicity and advertising follow). At one of your first meetings, discuss Dignity's Statement of Position and Purpose. This is a statement which many groups have found useful in giving their activities direction and purpose. It will also help focus discussion on the direction your new group should take.

One thing cannot be stressed too much. If you wish to do effective ministry, you must be welcoming and inclusive of all people who cane to your group. The best way to make everyone feel welcome is to get a good mix of people at your first meeting so that you have a variety of input. People of color will feel welcome if there are other people of color present. Women will feel a part of the group if other women are present. Don't depend on your own friends and acquaintances for those first few meetings. Make every outreach possible to see that you have a good mix from the very start. You will no doubt also find that people who are not Catholics may wish to be a part of the group; let them know that they are most welcome too. We are, after all, an organization of gay and lesbian Catholics and our friends!

You will need to make some decisions very quickly. Temporary officers will need to be chosen and you will need to obtain an address for the group (a post office box is usually best) and a telephone contact. Then you will need to appoint someone to plan liturgies, to do news releases to the local media and someone to open a bank account for chapter funds. For the protection of all concerned, we strongly recommend that you have a two signature account. By contacting your National Director you can obtain a supply of National Journals, Regional newsletters and membership applications. While it is not necessary to do all these things at your first few meetings, the sooner you do them, the quicker your Dignity chapter will be off to a good beginning

You will need to decide what type of activities to have at your meetings. Dignity has a strong religious focus so you may want to have some type of liturgy or prayer at some of your meetings. (Suggestions on Liturgy follow) You will want to have social activities and educational programs especially in smaller communities. Group discussion are also excellent ways of helping people to get to know one another. They are inexpensive and easy to organize compared with other activities. Some topics might include: '1Coming Out," "Experiences in the Church," "What my Faith means tome," "Problems in the Workplace," "Relationships with others" and "Sexual Ethics." Give everyone an opportunity to speak without having any one person dominate the conversation.

You might consider inviting people from other organizations to come speak to you group about a certain issue. Lesbian/gay political groups are quite willing to do so on occasion. You might also screen movies, which may be of interest to the group such as major releases with gay/lesbian themes, educational tapes and the like. Activities involving the outdoors are usually fun such as hikes, picnics, a day at the beach or mountains, a group trip to a football or baseball game or maybe a backyard barbecue at a member's home.

We suggest that you make contact with your neighboring chapters, become involved in Regional and Sub-regional activities whenever possible and work toward achieving full chapter status as soon as possible. The length of time to achieve full status will vary from region to region. In order to become a chartered chapter it will be necessary to have ten (10) dues paying members in your chapter and have bylaws which are approved by your chapter's members. Those bylaws should not be conflict with the Dignity STATEMENT OF POSITION AND PURPOSE nor Dignity 's national policies. When your National Director concurs that your group is ready for full chapter status, s/he will request a charter be granted at the next Dignity/USA board meeting.

We welcome you to the Dignity/USA family! We hope that these first few months of working toward full chapter status will be exciting and that your efforts will bear much fruit.

Good luck and remember God loves you more that you will ever know. May God be with us all.

Minimum Requirements

The following guidelines are in the Manual of the Board of Directors:

1. Ten or more PAID members
2. Acting in accordance with the Statement of Position and Purpose of DIGNITY/USA
3. Timely submission of assessments and report forms.
4. Having existed as a chapter-in-formation for a recommended six months.

Having a set of bylaws, rules and/or regulations not in conflict with or limiting, the Bylaws of DIGNITY/USA or the bylaws, rules and/or regulations of the region in which they are located.

TIME-TABLE

The timetable is based on the date of the Board of Directors Session on which the request of the chapter-in-formation is to be voted.

1) 6O days before the Board Session, the following must be submitted before this date:

A. A letter from the chapter-in-formation to the Board of Directors requesting chapter status. This should be accompanied by supporting evidence (Brag about what you've been doing'.). This is to be sent to the National Secretary with a carbon to your National Director in your Region.
B. A copy of your bylaws, rules and/or regulations are to be submitted to the National Secretary
C. A letter of recommendation from your National Director to the Board of Directors. This is to be sent to the National Secretary.

2) 45 days before the Board of Directors Session, you will receive one of three things from the National Secretary:

A. A letter from the National Secretary stating that the recommendation of the Officers and your National Director is to deny your request. You then have the right to appeal this recommendation to the entire Board of Directors either in writing or in person. Contact the National Secretary, especially if you will be appealing in person, immediately so that you are put on the agenda of the Board session.
B. A letter or phone call from the National Secretary alerting you to sane deficiency in your bylaws, rules and/or regulations that need adjusting or in your assessment and/or membership reporting that need to be clarified before a recommendation can be made by the Officers and National Director
C. Nothing. This is a clear indication that the recommendations are positive. (Sit back and relax!)

ADVICE

To avoid the first two results, the following is recommended:

I. Be sure your assessments are in on time and your membership reporting is up-to-date. Check with both the National Secretary and your National Director
2. Be sure your bylaws, rules and/or regulations are in order.
3. Brag about what you've been doing since you became a chapter-in-formation. Show that you are a strong chapter.
4. A request for chapter status is not a haphazard process. The sooner documentation is submitted, the easier it is for the deficiencies to be worked out and chapter status granted

ORGANIZATION

The basic unit of Dignity is the chapter. In order for Dignity to succeed, the individual chapters must be healthy, active and growing. In according with the bylaws of Dignity/USA, to be a chartered chapter of Dignity/USA, a chapter must have a minimum of ten (10) dues paying members, have existed continuously for at least six months, and have the recommendation of the National Director. Once the charter is granted the required number of members to maintain active status is not specified.

The individual chapters are organized into regions. Each region's geographical organization is indicated in the bylaws. The list of regions is as follows:

REGION GEOGRAPHICAL COMPOSITION

NORTHEAST New Jersey, New York

PACIFIC Alaska, California, Hawaii, Idaho, Nevada, Oregon, Washington

 

There are Dignity chapters in the United States and Dignity chapters in Canada. The chapters in Canada are part of Dignity/Canada/Dignite, Both Dignity/USA and Dignity/Canada/Dignite had their beginnings in San Diego & Los Angeles in 1969. By resolution of the House of Delegates in 1985, Dignity/Canada/Dignite became a separate organization from Dignity/USA. Through our International Liaison, Dignity/USA maintains contact with Dignity/Canada/Dignite and other lesbian/gay Catholic groups in France, England and Australia. Many states in the USA do not yet have a Dignity chapter. A current list of chapters of Dignity/USA is included in this manual.

Each region has a House of Delegates. The Regional House conducts the business of the region, and advises the National Director. In most regions, the Regional House plans regional {meetings and conferences and organizes other regional activities. The Regional House is composed of at least one delegate from each chapter in the region, and sees to its own finances. A portion of each member's dues (from the region) helps to finance the expenses of the Regional House.

The National Director is elected by the Regional House and has duties and responsibilities assigned by the Dignity/USA bylaws and the bylaws of that region. The Regional House of Delegates also elects one or more Regional Coordinators as needed to conduct the business of the region. The Regional Coordinators are responsible for the administration of regional business, for regional finances and responsible to the delegates for their performance. The National Director is responsible to the Regional Delegates for his/her performance on Dignity/USA's Board of Directors. In many regions, some of these duties are delegated to other officers of the region. A region may have a treasurer, secretary, newsletter editor or administrator, as indicated by the regional bylaws. One of the most important functions of the National Director is supervision and assistance to chapters-in-formation. In order for any chapter to receive a charter, the Director must recommend it. The director will also propose the revocation of a chapter1s charter when the chapter is no longer active (e.g. has dropped to zero membership), when the chapter requests revocation of the charter, or for cause, which is outlined specifically in the bylaws of Dignity/USA.

The National Board of Directors consists of the National Director from each of the seven regions and the National CWC Representative and the four officers of Dignity/USA. The Dignity/USA bylaws also provide for the election of three additional members of the board, as determined by the board to ensure diversity and continuity on the board.

The Board is responsible for the finances of Dignity/USA, for implementing "interim" policies of the organization and administering the policies of the House of Delegates. The officers of Dignity/USA are responsible for the day-today operation of the organization.

The House of Delegates of Dignity/USA is composed of delegates from all the chapters. The representation of the chapters is proportional as outlined in the USA bylaws. The House of Delegates meets every two years immediately prior to the Dignity/USA convention.

According to the bylaws of Dignity/USA, each chapter is entitled to at least one delegate. In addition, any chapter having more than 25 members on its official enrollment roster, as registered with the Secretary shall be entitled to additional delegates. As a rule, voting on issues takes place only at meetings of the House of Delegates. There have been votes on major topics between session of the House in which the votes were taken by mail.

The sample bylaws, included with this package, recommend certain officers for each chapter. These officers are only suggested. It is recommended, however, that the main leaders of your chapter become the delegates to the region and to the Dignity/USA convention.

The officers of Dignity/USA are President, Vice-President, Secretary and Treasurer. The House of Delegates elects the President and Vice-President for a two-year term during their biennial session, which occurs immediately before a Dignity/USA convention. The National Board of Directors elects the Secretary and Treasurer upon recommendation from the new President and Vice President of Dignity/USA. Gender parity must be maintained between the two top offices, that is, the offices of President and Vice-President must be held by one woman and one man. There is no provision in the bylaws of Dignity/USA concerning the number of terms officers may serve. Therefore, if the House of Delegates wishes, it may repeatedly elect the same slate of officers.

The officers conduct the day-to-day business of the organization, and serve as its corporate officers. Dignity/USA is a registered non-profit corporation in the District of Columbia. The President of Dignity/USA is also the official spokesperson for the organization. No one else is authorized to speak for Dignity/USA unless s/he has been so designated.

Sample Bylaws for Dignity/Anytown

I. NAME

The name of this organization shall be DIGNITY/ANYTOWN, hereafter referred to as the chapter.

II. PURPOSE AND REASON FOR EXISTENCE

A. The purpose and function of this chapter shall be to work to achieve those goals outlined in the Statement of Position and Purpose of Dignity Inc. (known as Dignity/USA) incorporated as part of these bylaws by this reference.

B. The organization may additionally undertake other purposes that do not contradict the above-mentioned statement.

III. OFFICERS AND GOVERNANCE

A. The chapter shall be governed by the following officers:

1. President

2. Vice-President

3. Secretary

4. Treasurer

B. Term of office for the above officers shall be one year and shall be served from the first of October to the thirtieth of September.

C. Rules for governance shall be as outlined in Robert's Rules of Order to the extent these rules do not contradict the bylaws of Dignity/USA.

D. Additionally, the chapter shall select Regional and National Delegates to represent the organization. The first two(2) delegates shall be the President and Vice-President of the chapter. Additional delegates shall be elected by the general membership. The terms of office of the delegates shall be identical with the terms of office of the officers of the chapter.

E. If either the President or Vice-President is unable to serve in the above capacity, that delegate's position shall be filled by a vote of the membership.

F. Elections of Officers shall be held annually during the month of September.

G. Officers shall be responsible for the levying of dues, appointing committees deemed necessary, fulfilling the purposes of the chapter and facilitating such other activities as may be necessary

H Officers may be removed from office for cause by two-thirds majority vote of the members in attendance at a regularly scheduled membership meeting.

IV. MEMBERSHIP

A. An individual becomes a member in good standing of the chapter by filling out a membership application and paying the appropriate dues.

B. Each member in good standing is eligible to vote in all elections of officers and delegates, and each member in good standing shall have the obligation of casting votes at meetings of the general membership.

V. MEETINGS

A. Meetings of the officers shall be held at least once per month for the purpose of conducting the ongoing business of the chapter.

B. Additional meetings of the officers my be held as called by the President, or by two other officers, on deliverance of a one (1) week notice to the other officers.

0. Meetings of the general membership shall be held at least semiannually. The dates of the membership meetings shall be announced at regular intervals beginning at least two (2) months before the meeting dates. A quorum of members shall be required to conduct business.

D. One of the meetings of the general membership shall coincide with the date of the election of officers in September

E. All meetings of the chapter shall be open to all that wish to attend, regardless of membership status. Only officers may vote at officers meetings and only members in good standing may vote at general membership meetings.

F. Anyone in attendance at a meeting shall be given the right to speak. Priority of speaking shall be given first to those eligible to vote.

G. All meetings shall be conducted in accordance with Robert's Rules of Order.

VI. EFFECTIVE DATE

These bylaws shall be effective on the day Dignity/Anytown is charted as a chapter of Dignity/USA. These bylaws will be executed by the provisional officers of the chapter, and will continue in effect until modified, altered, replaced or rescinded by a two-thirds vote of the membership at a regularly scheduled meeting of the general membership.

LITURGIES

Spirituality is one of the hallmarks of Dignity/USA and its chapters. Most chapters do sponsor same type of liturgical function either at all of their gatherings or on a regular basis.

if you've never planned a liturgy before, the concept may be a bit frightening to you at first. Remember, "liturgy" means "the work of the people." That means there can be as many kinds of liturgy as there are groups that gather together to worship. While many Dignity chapters celebrate Mass regularly, other types of liturgy are alternated with Mass and have become to many chapters, the principle form of worship. Many Catholic communities are exploring various kinds of prayer and celebration, which enrich the spiritual lives of the community. What is important is that the spiritual and liturgical life of the chapter meets the needs of the chapter's membership. This means not only that they are ministered to, but that they have the opportunity to minister to the community also.

There are many excellent resources to help in your planning. Visit any religious bookstore of any denomination and sample the material that is available. In addition to printed materials, you might also want to identify people in your community who can help. In many parishes, colleges and religious communities1 priests, sisters, brothers and lay people who regularly plan liturgies will be able to offer support and advice. They can also help you identify people who would preside at your liturgies, or train your own members to do so

When planning liturgy, keep in mind that Dignity was never intended to replace membership in the local parish. But know that for many of your members, Dignity will replace the parish and for each person, this as a very personal choice.

Do all that you can to gather your chapter together in prayer and celebration1 social events, projects, etc. Do all that you can to allow a vital faith communty of justice seeking friends to develop. The intimacy of your chapter will be evident in your liturgies. Chapters whose members know and care for one another prays together often and well.

For more help and ideas concerning liturgy and the formation of a faith community, contact your Regional Coordinator or National Director. S/he will help you and will also put you in touch with other individuals who can help you also.

FUNDRAISING

Every Dignity chapter is in need of additional funding and the best way to see that there is adequate money to meet the chapter's needs and goals is through fundraising. There are many ways to do this. Ultimately, the best method is to have each member give a certain percentage of his/her income on a regular basis. Tithing is becoming a concept more familiar to the American Catholic today. Some of your members would be willing to tithe to the chapter if encouraged to do so. Lately parishes have focused on a three-pronged approach of giving time, talent and treasures. In this way the donor is challenged to give of his/her full person, not just what is surplus. The following are other ideas to use for the raising of funds for the chapter:

* 50-50 raffle (probably the easiest to do and one of willing to give more that they would otherwise, if this the more lucrative, it also provides the instant reward for the winner and the winner is part of the event as it happens)

* Charge for a social event, dance, speaker or party

* Bake sale

* Rummage/white elephant/yard sale

* Raffle tickets for a specific purpose (this usually nets you what you need and then some, however, if you are incorporated and/or have tax-exempt status, your money is then locked into that specific purpose and may not be used for any other purpose-auditors will be looking for this)

* Fund appeal letter ( these are a little hard to do and require some overhead costs.)

* Conduct a time/talent series in your chapter that will increase donations and gifts (this requires some effort, commitment, and training for the folks who will be conducting the appeal.)

Finally, almost any public library in your city will have a section on volunteer organizations. There are a number of books available on grassroots fundraising for small organizations. Remember, the best policy is this; you have a cause you believe in it, you are committed to it and you make it fun.

ADVERTISING

Most chapters of Dignity do some form of advertising, whether it be strictly by word of mouth or by formal advertisements placed in various publications. Your chapter may decide to do some local advertising to help get the word out that you exist, and that you are a part of a national gay/lesbian Catholic organization.

Although advertising can be quite expensive sometimes it can be totally free, depending on the resources available in your area. You may be able to get some help for paid advertising from your Dignity region. Contact your Regional Coordinator or National Director (listed on the attached chapter list) for information on this possibility.

Many regularly published newspapers have "Events" sections which will run a listing of a meeting or liturgy at no cost. You must generally contact the newspaper for requirements (how big the listing must be, types of language to be used, whether a contact telephone number is required, etc.) Even in relatively small cities, few newspapers will deny your request to list.

There are usually gay/lesbian newspapers in cities and towns having a sizable gay/lesbian community. These papers will usually accept any "News Releases" regarding the activities of your chapter. If there is no gay/lesbian press n your community, check out the weeklies also available in your community. These papers often are published by people interested in the arts, and are generally available free to the public. They may charge something for publication of an ad, but usually run a news release gratis

If you have a local gay/lesbian community center, be sure you are listed as a resource on their hot line. Usually, you will only have to ask to be included, but you may wish to become a "business ~er1' of that organization in order to make your listing as a resource more readily accepted. If there is no community center, there may be a women's hot line or women's center. Traditionally, these centers have been very helpful in disseminating information about gay/lesbian organizations

Some radio stations and/or television stations in your area will accept PSA's (Public Service Announcements). Some of these may even have a gay/lesbian radio program which will happily list your chapter's activities as part of their local newscast. Usually they will ask that you prepare a 10 or 30 second "spot" to be read over the air

Don't overlook the possibility of advertising in the local Catholic paper. Even though the official Vatican line is firm, many diocesan justice and peace officers, ministries to singles, or ministries to the "spiritually alienated" will be happy to help publicize the fact that you exist and have regularly scheduled meetings and liturgies. Even the religion editor of your local mainstream newspapers may want to talk to someone from the chapter when some "newsy" catholic event is happening.

Finally, contact the owners and managers of businesses and organizations that cater to the gay/lesbian community in your area. These organizations (bars, bookstores, restaurants, clubs etc.) will usually be happy to let you place fliers or allow you to post a notice on their bulletin boards. Usually you can promise a "thank you" to the organization in your local newsletter as payment. Don't overlook these business resources as potential sponsors for fund raising activities

Feel free to contact your Regional Coordinator or National Director for additional help in finding sources for free advertising. The Director is eager to help you get your chapter off to a good start, and will give you as much help as he or she can. It may be possible for the Director to travel to your city to speak at rallies, meetings, or as a guest on a local talk show.
